# Doc_ID: asthma_management
# Title: Asthma control and inhaler-based therapy
# Journal: Demo Respiratory Review
# Year: 2022
# Source: Curated sample text

Asthma is a chronic inflammatory airway disease characterized by variable symptoms such as wheeze, cough, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. Good asthma care requires symptom assessment, trigger reduction, and appropriate inhaler therapy.

Inhaled corticosteroids reduce airway inflammation and are central to long-term asthma control for many patients. Reliever medication can help with acute symptoms, but frequent reliever use may indicate poor control and should prompt clinical review.

Patient education matters because inhaler technique, adherence, environmental triggers, and action plans all influence outcomes. Severe symptoms, blue lips, or difficulty speaking require urgent medical attention.
